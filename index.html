Game of Tag

In this assignment, I worked with Java objects, interfaces, nested classes, exceptions, and worked with multiple Threads. Additionally, I used HashMaps, ArrayLists, Strings, Scanner, and other basic Java constructs. The goal of the game was to have a user chase around a computer who randomly moved around until the user was able to tag them. The computer moved around the board laying traps and the user would win once the computer was caught.

Geography Project

Nature Deficit Disorder

Urbanization has changed our world today mostly for the better. It has allowed more creativity and large urban cities have become the driving force behind the economy. These urbanized areas have such a pull that a lot of people gravitate and move towards or within them. A problem with urbanization however is an increase in noise and lack of exposure to nature. Living in urbanized areas and being exposed to high decibels of sound leads to negative health effects. Some of which are an increase in blood pressure, your chance of getting a stroke, and heart attack chance. This all stems from higher stress which leads you to eat and drink more. This knowledge should make city planners take action. Some actions that some cities have taken are an increase in electric vehicles, design buildings in a noise absorbing way, and "nice" noise to counter the high volumes of noise. Ultimately, where we live makes a difference and finding more ways to alleviate stress should be on the minds of city planners. This project aims to find the places in the Twin Cities with the highest "natural" areas and the locations in the Twin Cities with the highest vulnerability.
